News and Events

Take a closer look at sustainability movements around the world!

Plastic pollution in our bodies of water and oceans has been a prevalent issue for years. One of the biggest plastics found is microfibres, a common synthetic fiber found in clothes. "Just a single load of synthetic laundry can release up to 18 million microfibers, "Dr.Anja Brandon says. Learn more about a California bill enacting filters on washing machines by clicking the link --> California bill

Glitter is a big proponent in polluting our earth, as it's made almost entirely of microplastics and contains toxic chemicals such as titanium dioxide. Another big problem with glitter-it's not biodegradable. That means all the microplastics in it will always be in the atmsophere, soil, and bodies of water. The fashion and makeup industries mass produce glitter which is problematic. However, many are looking for alternatives, click the link to learn more about a microplastic free glitter made by Eco Glitter Fun --> Sustainable Glitter

Many beauty companies use plastic packaging for their products, while packaging is necessary to ensure cleanliness and efficiency in a product it contains plastics which will pollute the planet. One alternatives to plastic packaging is glass. The brand Gisou, creates all their products with eco friendly packaging, and are manufactured through solar energy. Click the link to be trasported to their website --> Gisou

Fast fashion is the mass manufacturing of clothing in unethical ways, such as sweat shops and cheap labour. These sweat shops are often in developing countries, where poverty rates may be high. These fast fashion companies take advantage of workers. The opposite of this is slow fashion, it is a movement advocating for the injustices labourers face in fast faschion companies and spreads the enviremental impacts the fashion undustry has. To learn more visit this link from Earth.org --> Slow Fashion
