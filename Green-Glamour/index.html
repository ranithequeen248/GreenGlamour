What is fast fashion?

Fast fashion refers to the mass production of inexpensive and rapidly changing clothing styles that are designed to be worn for a short period of time before being discarded. This model of fashion has become popular in recent decades due to the demand for low-priced clothing that can be quickly replaced as trends change. However, the fast fashion industry has devastating effects on the environment. The production of these clothes requires large amounts of water, energy, and raw materials, resulting in significant greenhouse gas emissions and pollution. The clothes themselves are often made from synthetic fibers that do not biodegrade, leading to a massive buildup of waste in landfills. Additionally, the fast fashion model relies on the exploitation of cheap labor in developing countries, where workers are often paid low wages and forced to work in dangerous and unsanitary conditions.
